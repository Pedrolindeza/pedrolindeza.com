Parvalbumin Allergy Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Are you allergic to all fish?

A: Yes, my allergy is specific to parvalbumin, a protein found in fish muscles. This means I'm allergic to all types of fish.

Q: What about seafood?

A: Seafood often contains parvalbumin, so I am also allergic to seafood like shrimp, crab, and lobster.

Q: What happens if you eat fish?

A: If I consume fish, it can lead to allergic reactions which may range from mild symptoms like hives and itching to severe reactions like difficulty breathing.

Q: When did you find out about your allergy?

A: I discovered my allergy to parvalbumin when I had a reaction after consuming fish at the age of 1. It was confirmed through allergy testing.
